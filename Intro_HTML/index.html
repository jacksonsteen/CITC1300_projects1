Masking Tutorial for After Effects

How to open Rotobrush

First, you are going to want to either press (Alt+W) or click the button displayed in the image which is located in the top left section of After Effects. These will both open the Rotobrush tool and allow you to use it. Afterwards, you double click on the clip or image you want to mask.

Important things to know when using Rotobrush

  • Hold Left Click = Draw
  • Hold Alt + Left click = Erase
  • (Alt+5) Allows you to switch view between the mask and the full preview
  • (Alt+6) Allows you to see what is masked surrounded by a red region of what isn't masked

What do you do when you finish drawing your character mask?

Once your mask looks like the above image and it is to your satisfaction, press the spacebar to allow rotobrush to interpet the mask to match the remaining frames of the clip to your previously selected mask. If the interpreted frames mess up or glitch in a certain area, simply draw or erase on that frame to fix the mistake. You can go to effect controls afterwards to change the feather on the edges or reduce chatter on the edges for a more refined mask.
